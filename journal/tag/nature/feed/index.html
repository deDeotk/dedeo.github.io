Too many theologians have turned their attention away from questions of objective, metaphysical truth to matters of aesthetics, or moral sentiment, or psychology, culture, or history.

. . . What Christian theology needs now more than ever is its traditional, Scholastic backbone.

—Dr. Edward Feser, “Neither nature alone nor grace alone”

The crying need in Roman Catholic theology finds an analog in contemporary Reformed catholicism. We Protestants used to know our Plato, Aristotle, Augustine, and Thomas as well as our Roman interlocutors. Moreover, we used to be concerned with the nature of reality, the nature of truth, and the unity of knowledge. Perhaps it is time for us, along with Rome, to steady our spines with some corrective surgery from the great doctors of the church. Where to begin? Try here, here, here, here, and here, for starters.
