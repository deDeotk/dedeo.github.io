Professor Linda Zagzebski answers the question, “Do Catholic theologians need philosophy?”

Her answers apply with equal force to whether Reformed catholic theologians need philosophy. For to the degree that Reformed theologizing is insular, intramural, and disconnected not only from catholic theological insights but also non-theological perspectives on universal human questions, it tends less to human flourishing and more to Corinthianizing; less to the true, good, and beautiful, and more to the uninteresting.
