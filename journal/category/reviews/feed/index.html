]]> Today I reviewed A. G. Sertillanges’s The Intellectual Life. I wish someone would have introduced me to this classic before I started my academic journey through college, seminary, and now post-grad studies. Most of the pitfalls, deficiencies, and dead ends explained therein I have been learning all too well from that old schoolmaster of the hard knocks tradition and reaping in turn no small amount of lament for the lost time, letdowns, and anxieties I’ve caused myself and others from my missteps.

For those of us who come from family heritages rooted deeply (and proudly) in the artes serviles, I think it is not only fair to say but also needs to be said that choosing an intellectual vocation without the benefit of a father or other trusted guide who well knows the way to and the way of life within the artes liberales is fraught with all the dangers of the blind leading the blind on a bushwhack through Amazon. If one’s hindsight can benefit another’s foresight, let me say without hesitation: you need a guide for living an intellectual life; the “how to” part of this life does not just magically come down from heaven; it is acquired not infused knowledge.

He who dares walk through the dark jungle, then, and who sees the wisdom of consulting an experienced guide who carries a sharp machete and a bright torch will not be disappointed in perusing Sertillanges’s classic meditation on St. Thomas’s “Letter of Thomas Aquinas to Brother John on How to Study.”

]]> Laurence O’Donnell, Bavinckiana digitalia: A Review Essay, The Bavinck Review 4 (2013): 51–63.

In this essay I present the state of the art concerning digital tools for Bavinck studies. I wrote this primarily for two groups of Bavinck scholars: first, the ever-growing number of non-European and non-American readers who might not have immediate access to digital media developed primarily in the West; second, the scholar for whom digital media is not his or her forte but who wishes nevertheless to know what sort of tools are currently available. Additionally, I hope that these reflections spur further development both in software development and in academic institutional development related to Bavinck studies. I suggest that the Bavinck Institute (or, as other have suggested, worldwide Bavinck Institutes) might benefit from modeling its digitization efforts after similar initiatives such as the Kuyper Digital Library and the CCEL.
